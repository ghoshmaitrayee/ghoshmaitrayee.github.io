Teaching

Building independent scientific thinkers

My teaching and mentoring emphasize conceptual rigor, structured learning, and research-integrated instruction. I aim to help students move from learning scientific ideas to applying them independently with confidence, curiosity, and technical depth.

Teaching Philosophy

  • First-principles thinking: I emphasize deep conceptual understanding over memorization, especially in thermodynamics, quantum mechanics, and statistical mechanics.
  • Scaffolded learning: I guide students from structured examples toward independent reasoning and open-ended problem solving.
  • Active learning: I value workshop-style sessions, guided discussions, and collaborative problem solving to improve engagement and retention.
  • Inclusive pedagogy: I support students from diverse backgrounds by setting clear expectations, providing accessible resources, and creating multiple pathways for participation.
  • Assessment for growth: I use transparent rubrics and timely feedback so that assessment reinforces learning rather than simply measuring performance.
  • Research integration: I connect classroom ideas to real scientific questions in physical chemistry, materials under extreme conditions, and planetary science.
  • Mentoring toward independence: I view mentoring as an extension of teaching, beginning with structure and gradually fostering ownership, confidence, and scientific judgment.
Teaching focus: From structured learning to independent scientific practice.
