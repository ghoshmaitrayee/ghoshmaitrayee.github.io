My research explores matter under the most extreme conditions found in nature and the lab — bridging planetary science, fusion energy, and computational method development.

---

## 01 · Planetary Interiors & Mineral Physics

What happens deep inside Earth, Mars, Venus, and ice giants like Neptune? I simulate the behavior of iron, nickel, and minerals at the enormous pressures and temperatures found in planetary cores — conditions impossible to recreate in a lab. This work helps explain seismic signals, magnetic fields, and the internal structure of planets both in our solar system and beyond.

**Topics:** Earth & super-Earth inner core · Mars & Venus core conditions · Ice giant interiors · Diamond formation · Phase diagrams

**Key work:**
- Cooperative diffusion in BCC iron at Earth & super-Earth inner core conditions — *J. Phys.: Condens. Matter* (2023)
- High-pressure melt curve of nickel at Mars core conditions *(manuscript in preparation)*
- Diamond co-existence with hydrocarbons in ice giant interiors *(manuscript in revision)*

## 02 · Matter at Extreme Conditions

At pressures of millions of atmospheres and temperatures exceeding millions of degrees, familiar materials transform in unexpected ways — metals become transparent, insulators conduct electricity, and new phases of matter emerge. I study these transformations in oxides and other materials, characterizing how they melt, conduct heat and electricity, and interact with light at the edge of what we can theoretically model.

**Topics:** Multi-megabar pressures · Warm dense matter · Transport properties · Melt curves · Equation of state

**Key work:**
- Melting and transport properties of Al₂O₃ at extreme conditions — *Phys. Rev. B* (2024)
- Stability and phase transitions of MgO₂ near Earth's mantle *(manuscript in preparation)*
- Thermally induced structural metastability of BCC iron — *Phys. Rev. B* (2025)

## 03 · Nuclear Fusion & Energy Science

Inertial confinement fusion — compressing a tiny pellet of fuel with powerful lasers until it ignites — is one of humanity's most ambitious energy projects. I contribute by modeling the materials used in fusion targets: the capsule shells, foam microstructures, and resin coatings that must perform flawlessly under nanosecond implosions. Accurate equations of state and shock dynamics for these materials are critical to achieving ignition.

**Topics:** Inertial confinement fusion · Fusion target design · Foam microstructures · Shock dynamics · CHON resin EOS

**Key work:**
- First-principles EOS of CHON resin for inertial confinement fusion — *Phys. Rev. E* (2022)
- Laser-direct-drive fusion target design with high-Z pusher shell — *Phys. Rev. E* (2023)
- EOS and shock dynamics of foam materials at extreme temperatures *(manuscript in preparation)*

## 04 · Computational Methods & Machine Learning

The computational tools we use shape what science is possible. I develop and apply machine-learned force fields that combine the accuracy of quantum mechanics with the speed needed to simulate thousands of atoms over meaningful timescales. This enables simulations — like mapping a full melt curve or tracking diffusion across millions of timesteps — that would be computationally prohibitive with traditional DFT alone.

**Topics:** ML force field development · On-the-fly ML-MD · DFT & orbital-free DFT · Phonon calculations · HPC at NERSC

**Key work:**
- On-the-fly ML-MD for nickel at planetary core conditions — *Best Postdoc Talk, HEDS School 2025*
- ML force-field development for foam microstructures in fusion conditions *(in progress)*
- Pseudo Jahn-Teller effects in 2D materials via orbital vibronic coupling — *Bull. Mater. Sci.* (2018)
