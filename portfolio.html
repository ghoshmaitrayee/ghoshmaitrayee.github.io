My research explores matter under the most extreme conditions found in nature and the lab — bridging planetary science, fusion energy, and computational method development.

Theme 01
Planetary interiors & mineral physics

What happens deep inside Earth, Mars, Venus, and ice giants like Neptune? I simulate the behavior of iron, nickel, and minerals at the enormous pressures and temperatures found in planetary cores — conditions impossible to recreate in a lab. This work helps explain seismic signals, magnetic fields, and the internal structure of planets both in our solar system and beyond.

Theme 02
Matter at extreme conditions

At pressures of millions of atmospheres and temperatures exceeding millions of degrees, familiar materials transform in unexpected ways — metals become transparent, insulators conduct electricity, and new phases of matter emerge. I study these transformations in oxides and other materials, characterizing how they melt, conduct heat and electricity, and interact with light at the edge of what we can theoretically model.

Theme 03
Nuclear fusion & energy science

Inertial confinement fusion — compressing a tiny pellet of fuel with powerful lasers until it ignites — is one of humanity's most ambitious energy projects. I contribute by modeling the materials used in fusion targets: the capsule shells, foam microstructures, and resin coatings that must perform flawlessly under nanosecond implosions. Accurate equations of state and shock dynamics are critical to achieving ignition.

Theme 04
Computational methods & machine learning

The computational tools we use shape what science is possible. I develop and apply machine-learned force fields that combine the accuracy of quantum mechanics with the speed needed to simulate thousands of atoms over meaningful timescales. This enables simulations — like mapping a full melt curve or tracking diffusion across millions of timesteps — that would be computationally prohibitive with traditional DFT alone.
